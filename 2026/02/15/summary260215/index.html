Weekly Global Market Summary 02/15/2026

This week, global markets followed a trajectory from early optimism to heightened risk-aversion, eventually settling into a cautious recovery. The primary narrative revolved around the cooling of US economic data and a structural shift in how the market values AI-driven innovation.

U.S. Markets: Sentiment Rollercoaster

The week began with a “risk-on” sentiment as semiconductor and software sectors saw bargain-hunting. However, the mood soured mid-week. The weak retail sales data on Tuesday dampened investor confidence. And the strong non-farm payrolls on Wednesday signaled that the Federal Reserve might delay rate cuts, putting pressure on tech and valuation-sensitive sectors.

Also, a significant upgrade from AI unicorn Anthropic triggered fears that AI could soon replace professional services in software, legal, and trading sectors. This “creative destruction” sparked a sharp tech sell-off and a subsequent liquidation cascade in the crypto market.

Fortunately, on Friday, cooling CPI data suggesting easing inflation and a strong Consumer Sentiment reading helped stabilize the market. The result was a rotation into traditional value stocks, with the Dow Jones closing at a historic 50,000 milestone, but tech indices remained under pressure.

The higher expectation on Fed interest rate cut and the in-flow due to the risk-off mood during the mid-week together pushed the yield to go down further.

European Markets: Defensive Resilience

European markets again remained closely linked to U.S. sentiment but proved more resilient during the mid-week tech rout. The heavy weighting of energy and defensive sectors—such as healthcare and financials—provided a necessary “buffer”. Additionally, European bonds performed strongly as cooling inflation data bolstered expectations for regional monetary easing.

Japan: The LDP’s Victory

The Japanese market stood out this week, driven by a definitive political shift: the Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) secured more than two-thirds of the seats in the House of Representatives. This landslide victory provides a clear mandate for Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi’s “Sanaenomics” platform of aggressive fiscal expansion and tax cuts.

The Nikkei 225 surged as investors bet on continued stimulus and a stable political environment. Interestingly, the decisive win also reduced market anxiety over “fiscal mismanagement,” leading to a slight appreciation in the Yen and higher JGB yields as expectations for disciplined expansion grew.

Commodities: Safe-Havens

The gold price continued its upward trajectory, supported by a weakening Dollar and persistent safe-haven demand.

The oil price faced downward pressure due to a combination of easing U.S.-Iran tensions, lowered demand growth forecasts from the IEA, and reports that OPEC+ might increase production earlier than expected.
