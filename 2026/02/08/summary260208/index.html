U.S. Markets: From AI Panic to the Value Pivot

US Market’s performance this week was volatile, shifting the primary narrative from macro policy to industrial “creative destruction”.

Mid-week, the AI unicorn Anthropic released a massive capability upgrade. Unlike previous “rising tide” cycles, this “mind-blowing” update triggered deep-seated fears of massive displacement in software services, data processing, and professional fields like law and trading. Because these sectors are pillars of the tech index, the innovation paradoxically led to a sharp tech sell-off.

On Friday, a strong Consumer Sentiment reading successfully reversed the gloom. Capital showed a distinct “structural rotation”—exiting high-valuation tech names and flowing into traditional value sectors. This propelled the Dow Jones Industrial Average to close above the historic 50,000 mark for the first time in its 129-year history.

Treasuries acted as a mirror to risk appetite, strengthening during the mid-week panic and consolidating as sentiment recovered, ending slightly stronger overall compared to last week.

Crypto: The Bithumb “Fat Finger” & Tech-Sector Spillover

The cryptocurrency sector was hit by a double-whammy this week, as macro tremors from the tech sector collided with one of the largest operational blunders in crypto history.

On February 6, South Korean exchange Bithumb suffered a massive “fat finger” error during a “Random Box” promotional event. Instead of distributing the intended 2,000 Korean Won (~$1.40) rewards, a staff member mistakenly selected BTC as the unit. This resulted in approximately 620,000 “phantom” Bitcoins—valued at over $44 billion—being credited to nearly 700 users.

As lucky recipients rushed to liquidate their windfalls, Bitcoin’s price on Bithumb suffered a 17% flash crash, briefly dipping to $56,000 while global markets traded near $70,000.

This volatility, arriving just as tech-sector tremors were already weighing on risk assets, triggered a massive wave of forced liquidations across global crypto markets. High-leverage long positions were wiped out, briefly intensifying a broader “risk-off” mood that saw Bitcoin tumble toward the low-$60,000s globally.

European Markets: Defensive Resilience

Unlike the tech-heavy Nasdaq, European indices are heavily weighted toward financials, mining, healthcare, and consumer staples. these defensive sectors acted as a crucial anchor during the week’s turbulence.

Japanese Markets: The “Sanae Takaichi” Mandate

The logic in Japan remained centered on the February 8th snap election.

Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi secured a landslide victory, with her coalition winning a supermajority in the lower house. Heading into the vote, the market’s “Takaichi Trade” intensified as investors bet on the continuation of her proactive fiscal expansion (Sanaenomics), including a ¥21 trillion stimulus package and potential consumption tax exemptions. This expectation provided a massive boost to the Nikkei 225—which surged toward record highs near 56,000—even as it fueled further Yen depreciation and pushed JGB yields to 27-year highs.

Commodities: Rebounded Gold and Oscillated Oil

After last week’s “Warsh Shock” caused a deep correction, the capital began to flow back into the gold market, driving a rapid rebound. Recurring geopolitical tensions provided additional safe-haven support.

Oil prices continued to swing based on the temperature of U.S.-Iran relations. With no immediate signs of substantial escalation this week, prices oscillated within a narrow range, closing slightly weaker than the previous week.
