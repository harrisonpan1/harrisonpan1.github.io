Macro Drivers:

  • ISM Manufacturing PMI prints 54.0 (previous 52.7, consensus 53), marking a near four-year high.
    • It proved that aggregate demand for Fortune 500-level heavy industry remains incredibly robust. Consequently, front-end rates reacted instantly: the 2-Year U.S. Treasury Yield (US02Y) gapped higher, while overnight index swaps (OIS) aggressively priced out the probability of a September rate cut, leaving only one full cut anticipated for the remainder of 2026.
  • Kevin Warsh as the new Federal Reserve Chairman.
    • Waller’s strict baseline set a formidable hawkish tone. Along with the blowout ISM data, the market priced in a highly proactive “Higher-for-Longer” regime under Chairman Warsh, leading to a violent bear steepening of the yield curve.
  • President Trump formally signed a presidential proclamation today altering tariff frameworks on strategic metals.
    • This structural incentive to buy American industrial components will push up the US manufacturing input cost in the mid-long term, adding long-term sticky inflation expectations (term premia) to long-end yields. On the other hand, it provides fundamental support to cyclical sectors, causing Materials (XLB) and Industrials (XLI) to heavily outperform defensive sectors.
  • An unconditional transit proposal aimed at normalizing international commercial shipping through the Strait of Hormuz with zero extra taxation or harassment.
    • This acted as a vital release valve for global risk assets. Coming on the heels of the weekend OPEC+ decision to gradually phase out voluntary production cuts, the easing of the Iranian conflict completely deflated the geopolitical risk premium in energy markets, alleviating fears of an acute, secondary inflation spike, allowing long-duration growth equity and secular AI names to stage a late-day recovery and defend historical highs.

Week Ahead Watchlist
