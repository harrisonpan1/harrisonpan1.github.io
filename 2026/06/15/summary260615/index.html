Rates

For the past three months, markets have been trading around one central risk, Iran war, which caused the Strait of Hormuz disruption and thus oil spike. Over this weekend, however, a preliminary US-Iran peace agreement was announced, with plans to reopen the Strait of Hormuz and end the conflict.

Markets immediatedly began removing a large amount of geopolitical risk premium. Oil collapsed more than 5% and the fear of inflation faded away. Investors moved back towards “Fed on Hold” which was already been priced before. Thus, the treasury yields barely moved today.

Equity

As the inflation fears decay, stock, especially growth stock, rallied strongly.

FX

The dollar weakened as inflation fears fall and the risk appetite back.

Commodity

As mentioned about, the oil rallied due to the removal of geopolitical risk.

As the expectation of inflation drops, the real yields fell, which boost the gold price today.

Crypto

Bitcoin broke back above $65,000 and participated fully in today’s risk rally.

Cross-Asset Read

  • Oil ↓ - Geopolitical premium removed
  • Treasury yields stay the same - Market expectation meets
  • Dollar ↓ - Less hawkish Fed pricing
  • Gold ↑ - Real yields falling
  • Equity ↑ - Risk appetite back
  • Bitcoin ↑ - liquidity back

The market is no longer debating on “Will inflation rise because of oil?” but instead on “How quickly can the inflation shock reverse if oil normalizes?”
