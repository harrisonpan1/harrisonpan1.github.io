What Happened to Silver This Year?

While mainstream investors were patting themselves on the back for a respectable 14% return on the S&P 500 in 2025, a quiet revolution was taking place in the commodities sector. Silver—often dismissed as “Gold’s little brother”—exploded by 170% within a single year, outperforming gold by more than double.

This was not a simple cyclical recovery. It was a “perfect storm” woven from structural shortages, geopolitical maneuvering, and high-leverage algorithmic warfare.

The “1:200” Shadow Game: How Leverage Built a Throne for Silver

The core mechanism behind silver’s insanity lies in a highly opaque market structure that expanded paper claims far beyond physical reality.

  • Physical-to-Paper Conversion: Speculators borrowed physical silver from miners, pledged it to banks for liquidity, and used that capital to go long on the market.
  • The Re-hypothecation Loop: Banks took those pledged certificates and lent them again to ETF issuers. This “multi-link” pledging resulted in a derivatives market that expanded silver’s exposure to more than 1:200 ratio against actual physical bars.
  • The Fatal Squeeze: When “Whales” demanded physical delivery, shorts found themselves in a vacuum. By October 2025, silver lease rates spiked to 30%, briefly hitting 100% in the London market as shorts scrambled to avoid default.

The Structural Deadlock: Supply and Demand Imbalance

The fundamental floor for this price surge is a supply-demand mismatch that has finally reached a breaking point.

Global silver has faced a supply deficit for five consecutive years.

Furthermore, silver’s industrial profile has changed as it is the most conductive metal on earth. In the AI era, it has become indispensable for high-performance chips and data center infrastructure while the solar industry’s shift to TOPCon cells has increased silver loading by up to 50% per unit.

Finally, a strategic revaluation has taken hold as the U.S. added silver to its critical minerals list, while China implemented export licensing in 2026, signaling the end of the era of cheap, abundant silver.

Geofinancial Drivers: India, Russia, and the JPMorgan Migration

The acceleration in late 2025 was catalyzed by deep geopolitical coupling and a major shift in institutional behavior. In October 2025, mysterious Indian buyers placed a delivery order for 1,000 tons in London, instantly draining New York’s available spot inventory and suggesting that silver was being used as a medium to help Russia convert “trapped” oil rupees into tangible bullion. This movement effectively tested a quasi-Silver Standard for cross-border settlements.

Simultaneously, JPMorgan, silver’s largest historical market maker, effectively flipped the table by moving their trading desk to Singapore to align with the Asian pivot. They engaged in aggressive physical take-delivery, shifting hundreds of tons from “eligible” to “locked” status and joining the squeeze they once managed.

2026 Outlook

As we exit 2025, silver hovers near the $80/oz mark. What’s next?

  • The “Ghost Week” Surge: During the liquidity vacuum between Christmas and New Year (Ghost Week), roughly 145,000 futures contracts were locked. With no sellers in sight, even minimal buying pressure triggered massive price gaps.
  • The Long-term Bull Case: Since gold reaches $4,500, based on gold-to-silver historical extreme ratio, 15:1, the theoretical target for silver could reach $300.
  • The Pullbacks Warning: Silver’s volatility is typically double that of gold. Expect technical pullbacks in early 2026 due to passive fund rebalancing (BCOM index adjustments) and potential hawkish pivots from the Fed.
