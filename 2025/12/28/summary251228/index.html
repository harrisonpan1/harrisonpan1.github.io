Weekly Global Market Summary 12/28/2025

As we approach the final stretch of 2025, the global market is being shaped by two seasonal forces: the “Santa Claus Rally” and “Window Dressing”.

  • The Santa Claus Rally typically sees light trading volume and reduced short-selling pressure, making it easier for prices to move upward on even modest buying.
  • The Window Dression happens when institutional managers start to prun underperformers and double down on high-conviction themes like AI to polish their annual reports.

Here is the deep-dive breakdown of the global market logic for this week.

U.S. Market: Holiday Effects vs. Mixed Macro Data

Macroeconomic data this week offered a mixed view about the economy and the impact is limited under this holiday market

  • GDP Surprise: U.S. Q3 GDP growth was revised to 4.3%, significantly beating forecasts. Theoretically, it reduces the urgency for Fed rate cuts
  • Soft CB Consumer Confidence: The actual number is 89.1, lower than previous 92.9.
  • Mixed Jobless Report: The Initial Jobles Claims fall while Continuing Jobless Claims is higher than previous.

Thus, the market chose to focus on capital flows rather than macro shifts. Funds continue to rotate into the AI sector, reinforcing the trend where the winners of 2025 keep winning as the year closes.

Japan: Leader of The Global Bond Ripple

The ripple effect from the Bank of Japan (BOJ) continues to be felt across global fixed-income markets.

Following last week’s symbolic rate hike to 0.75%, the Yen surprisingly weakened rather than strengthened. This raised fears of imported inflation, putting pressure on the BOJ to tighten further in 2026. Combined with concerns over expanded fiscal budgets and increased bond supply, Japan’s 10-year yield surged past 2.1%, its highest since 1999.

While the “JGB Shock” initially dragged down U.S. and European bond prices, the U.S. Treasury market found support. A solid 7-year note auction proved that demand remains robust at these yield levels, allowing U.S. Treasuries to end the week slightly stronger despite the global pressure. And the European bonds recovered due to holiday-driven buying.

Europe: A Holiday Break

With most European markets open for only two and a half days due to the Christmas holidays, stocks saw a mild “Santa Rally” but on very thin volume, characterized more by sector rotation than broad directional moves.

Commodities: Crazy Gold & Silver

Gold and Silver continue to be the standout assets of late 2025, with Gold hitting new historic highs above $4,400/oz due to the following reasons:

  • Safe-Haven Demand: Intensifying geopolitical risks—specifically U.S. sanctions on Venezuelan oil and escalated drone activity in the Russia-Ukraine conflict—drove investors into bullion.
  • The “Debasement Trade”: As investors grow wary of rising sovereign debt levels and a weakening U.S. dollar, they are rotating out of bonds and into precious metals as a hedge against currency debasement.
  • Silver’s Industrial Edge: Silver outpaced gold this week. As a “dual-purpose” metal, it benefited both from the precious metals rally and its critical role in the AI infrastructure buildout (data centers) and green energy.

Crude Oil remained the outlier, stuck in a tug-of-war between supply disruptions and expectations of a global surplus in 2026, ending the week largely flat.
